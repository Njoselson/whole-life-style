# Creditor Report — Why This Cooperative Is Creditworthy

## Executive Summary

A member-owned cooperative with $150K in pooled capital and $1M+ in term life death benefit collateral seeks a lending relationship. All death benefits are collateral-assigned to the cooperative via standard AMA/ACLI forms with A-rated carriers. The cooperative lends to members at 0% interest, backed by death benefit collateral that self-liquidates on the triggering event.

## Entity Structure

- **Parent**: Mutual Benefit Corporation (future 501(c)(8) Fraternal Benefit Society)
- **Property Holding LLC**: Single-purpose entity for real estate (clean underwriting)
- **Cooperative Pool**: Operating account, member contributions, lending

No Life Insurance LLC. All policies are individually owned term life, collateral-assigned to the cooperative.

## Collateral: Death Benefit

Every member carries term life insurance collateral-assigned to the cooperative.

| Member Type | Coverage | Members | Total |
|------------|---------|---------|-------|
| Founders | $500K each | 2 | $1,000,000 |
| Regular members | $100K each | 50 | $5,000,000 |
| **Total death benefit** | | **52** | **$6,000,000** |

### Why Death Benefit Is Superior Collateral

1. **Guaranteed by A-rated insurers** — not subject to market depreciation
2. **Self-liquidating** — on the triggering event (death), the collateral pays out automatically
3. **No foreclosure, no auction, no depreciation** — just a check from the insurance company
4. **Actuarially predictable** — mortality rate at age 35 is ~0.1%/year. Expected deaths in 52-member pool over 10 years: ~0.5
5. **Collateral assignment is standard** — AMA/ACLI forms, recognized by all lenders and courts

### Collateral Assignment Terms

- Cooperative is collateral assignee on all member policies
- On death: cooperative takes only outstanding obligations, remainder to beneficiaries
- On default: cooperative retains assignment until obligations are settled
- On departure: assignment released after all obligations are met
- Assignments filed with insurance carriers, written acknowledgments on record

## Risk Analysis

### Credit Risk (Member Loans)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Member defaults on loan | Pool equity seized; loss spread across all members |
| Member dies with outstanding loan | Death benefit pays loan in full — zero loss |
| Mass default | Credit limits are 5× contributions; member has skin in the game |
| Member borrows and disappears | Gradual credit unlock (5× contributions) prevents day-1 max borrowing |

### Credit Limit Model

Members cannot borrow unlimited amounts. Credit unlocks gradually:

- **Formula**: min(total_contributions × 5, coverage × 75%)
- **Regular member at month 12**: $240 contributed → $1,200 credit limit
- **Regular member at month 60**: $1,200 contributed → $6,000 credit limit
- **Maximum**: $75,000 (75% of $100K death benefit)

A member would need to contribute $15,000 to reach the $75K cap. By that point, they have significant skin in the game.

### Property Risk

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Housing member stops paying | 90-day cure period → forced buyout → eviction → unit re-assigned |
| Vacancy | Waitlist of non-housing members with housing priority |
| Property depreciation | Limited equity model caps speculation; CLT option preserves affordability |
| Mortgage default | Death benefit collateral + pool reserves + member contribution stream |

### Actuarial Risk

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Annual mortality rate (age 35) | 0.10% |
| Expected deaths per year (52 members) | 0.05 |
| Expected death benefit payout per year | $6,000 |
| Probability of 2+ deaths in one year | < 0.1% |
| Total death benefit | $6,000,000 |

Even if 2 members die in one year: $5.77M in collateral remains. The portfolio effect makes this extremely safe.

## Legal Framework

### Governing Documents
- **Operating Agreement**: member tiers, capital return model, equity formula, voting rights, reserves
- **Buy-Sell Agreement**: death, disability, departure, forced buyout — all exit scenarios covered
- **Collateral Assignment Forms**: standard AMA/ACLI, filed with each insurance carrier
- **Member Applications**: financial disclosure, contribution commitment

### Regulatory Path
- Currently: mutual benefit corporation (state-chartered)
- At 25+ members: 501(c)(8) fraternal benefit society (tax-exempt, insurance authority)
- Future: CDFI certification (federal grants, technical assistance)

### Securities Analysis
- Founder lump sums are **zero-interest loans** to the cooperative (capital return, not equity)
- Members are **active participants** (governance, voting, committees) — not passive investors
- No profit sharing, no dividends, no guaranteed returns
- Structure designed to avoid Howey test classification as investment contract
