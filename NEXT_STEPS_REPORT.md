## The Model: Term-Only Cooperative

**What it is**: You and your wife contribute $150K to the cooperative. Everyone — founders and regular members — pays $20/mo. All members carry term life insurance. The $150K is the down payment on a brownstone. 4 members live there and pay carrying charges. The cooperative uses term life death benefit as collateral to establish creditworthiness. Zero-interest loans from the pool.

**Why term-only**:
- Permanent life costs ~$650/mo combined for founders — drains the pool
- Term life costs ~$90/mo combined for founders ($500K coverage each) — pool stays healthy
- No Life Insurance LLC needed — simpler legal structure, fewer entities
- No cash value to manage — death benefit is the only collateral needed
- Pool grows from contributions, not from insurance premiums cycling through

**Two member types**:

| | Housing Member | Non-Housing Member |
|--|---------------|-------------------|
| Monthly cost | $20/mo + ~$1,588 carrying | $20/mo only |
| Life insurance | $100K term | $100K term |

| Property equity | ~$20K per-unit share (5 yrs) | N/A (future priority) |
| Zero-interest loans | Up to $75K credit | Up to $75K credit |
| Credit unlocks at | 5× contributions | 5× contributions |

## Founder Equity: Capital Return, Not Ownership

The $150K is a **zero-interest loan** to the cooperative, not a purchase of equity. Founders get their money back over 5 years via vesting (20%/year).

**Capped return**: Founders can never get back more than $150K. The cooperative owns all growth.

| Year | Founder Gets Back | Vested % |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| 1 | ~$28K capital + common | 20% |
| 2 | ~$58K capital + common | 40% |
| 3 | ~$89K capital + common | 60% |
| 4 | ~$119K capital + common | 80% |
| 5 | ~$150K capital + common | 98% |

**Common equity**: All members earn common equity weighted by **time** (member-months), not money. A member who's been in for 60 months has more common equity than one who joined at month 24. Early members (months 1-12) get a 1.5× bonus on their time weight.

## Credit Limit Model

Every $1 you contribute unlocks $5 in credit, capped at 75% of your death benefit.

| Scenario | Contributed | Credit Limit | Max Credit |
|----------|------------|--------------|------------|
| Regular at month 12 | $240 | $1,200 | $75,000 |
| Regular at month 60 | $1,200 | $6,000 | $75,000 |
| Founder ($150K lump, month 1) | $150,020 | $375,000 | $375,000 |
| Member with $5K lump, month 1 | $5,020 | $25,100 | $75,000 |
| Member with $50K lump, month 1 | $50,020 | $75,000 | $75,000 |

**Why this is safe**: Credit is backed by death benefit. If a member dies, insurance pays the loan. If they default, the cooperative seizes pool equity and can absorb the rest across all members. The gradual unlock prevents someone from joining, borrowing $75K on day 1, and disappearing.

**Other couples can contribute lump sums** too — gets them higher credit limits immediately. A couple that puts in $50K on day 1 gets $75K in credit (hits the $100K×75% cap). They don't need to wait 750 months of $20/mo to reach that.

## Legality Assessment

### What's Proven (Low Risk)
- **Collateral assignment of life insurance**: Standard practice. Every bank does this.
- **Cooperative/mutual benefit corporations**: Well-established in all 50 states.
- **Term life insurance pools**: Fraternal benefit societies have done this for 150+ years.
- **Zero-interest member loans**: Not unusual in cooperatives (no usury concern since rate is 0%).

### What Needs an Attorney (Medium Risk)
- **501(c)(8) formation**: Requires IRS application, state insurance department approval.
- **Capital return model**: Must be documented as a loan to the cooperative, not equity.
- **Time-weighted equity**: Non-standard for cooperatives — operating agreement must be clear.
- **Credit limit model**: Must comply with Equal Credit Opportunity Act and state lending laws.

### What's Simpler vs. Whole Life Model
- No Life Insurance LLC → one fewer entity, no partnership taxation complexity
- No transfer-for-value risk → no permanent life policy transfers
- No cash value management → simpler accounting
- Founder contribution is a loan, not equity → cleaner securities analysis
