# Collateral Assignment Outline

## 1. What Is Collateral Assignment

- Conditional assignment of life insurance policy rights to the cooperative (and/or lender)
- Assignee has claim on death benefit and/or cash value in event of default or death
- Distinct from absolute assignment: cooperative does NOT own the policy
- Member retains ownership, beneficiary designation rights, and premium payment obligation
- Assignee entitled only to outstanding loan/obligation amount

## 2. Term Life Collateral Assignment (Tier 1 & 2 Members)

### What Is Assigned
- Death benefit up to the amount of member's share of outstanding cooperative loans
- No cash value exists (term life has no cash value)

### Process
1. Member obtains term life policy ($100K coverage minimum)
2. Member signs AMA/ACLI standard collateral assignment form
3. Form filed with insurance company (company acknowledges in writing)
4. Cooperative named as assignee (not beneficiary — important distinction)
5. Insurance company notified of assignment and acknowledges

### Release Conditions
- Member has no outstanding obligation to cooperative
- Member departs cooperative per buy-sell agreement
- Cooperative loan fully repaid
- Board resolution authorizing release
- Written release filed with insurance company

### Member Obligations
- Keep policy active (pay premiums on time)
- Notify cooperative of any policy changes
- Not assign policy to any other party without cooperative consent
- Maintain minimum coverage amount ($100K)

## 3. Permanent Life Collateral Assignment (Tier 3 Members)

### What Is Assigned
- Death benefit up to outstanding loan/obligation amount
- Cash value (up to 90% available for collateral loans)
- Cash value serves as secondary collateral for property loans

### Additional Provisions
- Policy loans against cash value require cooperative consent
- Cash value withdrawal restricted during active collateral assignment
- Cooperative tracks cash value growth for collateral adequacy
- Annual policy statement required from insurance company

### Dual Assignment (when lender requires)
- Primary: lender holds first position on death benefit for mortgage amount
- Secondary: cooperative holds second position for remaining obligations
- Clear priority agreement between cooperative and lender
- Notification requirements to both parties

## 4. Lender Notification Requirements

- Lender notified of all collateral assignments at loan origination
- Annual certification that policies remain active and assignments in force
- Immediate notification if:
  - A policy lapses or is at risk of lapsing
  - A member departs the cooperative
  - Assignment is released or modified
  - Premium payment is missed (grace period notification)
- Lender may require minimum aggregate death benefit covenant

## 5. Insurance Company Requirements

- Written acknowledgment of assignment from each carrier
- Carrier must be A-rated or better (AM Best, S&P, Moody's)
- Diversify across 2-3 carriers to reduce concentration risk
- State guaranty association coverage: up to $300K per policy in most states
- Annual policy status confirmation

## 6. Documentation Checklist

- [ ] Collateral assignment form (per policy, per member)
- [ ] Insurance company acknowledgment letter
- [ ] Lender notification letter (if applicable)
- [ ] Board resolution authorizing assignment
- [ ] Member consent and signature
- [ ] Policy summary (coverage amount, premium, carrier, policy number)
- [ ] Assignment release form (for use upon exit)
