# Loan Application Package Outline

## What the Lender Needs to See

This document outlines the complete package for approaching lenders with a non-traditional cooperative structure backed by life insurance collateral.

## 1. Business Plan

### Executive Summary
- Cooperative structure and purpose
- Member count and growth trajectory
- Insurance portfolio summary (term + permanent)
- Property target and acquisition strategy
- Revenue model (carrying charges + rental income)

### Organizational Structure
- Fraternal Benefit Society [501(c)(8)] parent entity
- Life Insurance LLC (partnership taxed, holds permanent policies)
- Property Holding LLC (clean entity for mortgage underwriting)
- Governance: board, officers, voting structure

### Member Profile
- Total members and tier breakdown
- Average credit score (target: 650+ minimum)
- Contribution history (months of on-time payments)
- Employment/income verification summaries
- Age distribution and insurability status

### Growth History
- Timeline from formation to current membership
- Contribution track record (demonstrated via ledger)
- Credit score improvements (MAF model metrics)
- Membership retention rate

## 3. DSCR Calculations

### Traditional DSCR
```
DSCR = Annual NOI / Annual Debt Service
Target: >= 1.25
```

### Adjusted DSCR (Member Contribution Model)
```
Adjusted DSCR = (Annual NOI + Annual Member Contributions) / Annual Debt Service
```
- Explains to lender that cash flow comes from member contributions, not just property NOI
- Demonstrates member capacity to service debt
- Shows diversified income sources (52+ contributing members)

## 6. Risk Mitigation Documentation

### Member Default Risk
- Buy-sell agreement with cure periods and forced buyout provisions
- Vesting schedule incentivizes long-term commitment
- Credit bureau reporting creates accountability
- Diversified membership (52+ members, no single point of failure)

### Insurance Lapse Risk
- Premiums paid from contributions before pool allocation
- 6-month premium reserve maintained
- Automatic premium payment via ACH
- Multiple carriers (A-rated) reduce concentration risk

### Property Risk
- Standard property insurance (replacement cost)
- Maintenance reserve fund
- Professional property management (optional)

### Buyout Funding Risk
- Death benefit covers death scenario buyouts
- Cash value covers living buyouts (Tier 3)
- Reserve fund covers gaps
- Installment plans for shortfalls

## 7. Lender Selection Strategy (per lender analysis Section 6.2)

### Target Lender Types
1. Community banks (relationship-based, flexible underwriting)
2. CDFIs (mission-aligned, understand cooperative structures)
3. Credit unions (member-focused, cooperative heritage)
4. SBA-approved lenders (504 program for commercial RE)
5. State housing finance agencies

### Avoid
- Large national banks with rigid automated underwriting
- Lenders unfamiliar with non-traditional collateral
- Lenders requiring traditional DSCR only (no adjusted model)

### Negotiation Points
- Propose collateral assignment as primary risk mitigant
- Offer loan covenants (minimum cash value, member count, premium compliance)
- Demonstrate coverage ratio (death benefit / loan amount)
- Show adjusted DSCR with member contribution model
