# Operating Agreement Outline — Whole Life Style Fraternal Benefit Society

## Article I: Formation and Purpose

- Entity type: Fraternal Benefit Society [501(c)(8)]
- State of formation
- Common bond definition (community, neighborhood, or professional)
- Purpose: provide life insurance benefits, pool capital for property acquisition, build member creditworthiness

## Article II: Member Classes and Tiers

### Tier 1: Base Member ($20/mo)
- Term life insurance ($100K coverage, group rate)
- Voting rights (one member, one vote)
- Pro-rata pool equity
- Credit bureau reporting
- Access to group borrowing power

### Tier 2: Housing Member ($20/mo + carrying charges)
- All Tier 1 benefits
- Unit in cooperative property
- Carrying charges cover share of: mortgage, taxes, insurance, maintenance
- Share purchase required (portion of down payment)

### Tier 3: Founder/Whale ($20/mo + lump sum)
- All Tier 1 benefits
- Higher term life coverage ($500K)
- Zero-interest loan to cooperative (returned via vesting)
- Board seat eligibility
- Higher credit limit (75% of $500K = $375K)

## Article III: Contribution Schedules

- Monthly contribution: $20/mo minimum for all tiers
- Lump sum contributions: any amount for Tier 3
- Premium allocation from contributions:
  - Tier 1/2: term life premium (~$9.10/mo group rate at age 35)
  - Tier 3: term life premium for higher coverage (~$45/mo at age 35 for $500K)
- Remainder to cooperative pool
- Automatic ACH collection required

## Article IV: Equity Formula — Capital Return + Time-Weighted Equity Model

### 4.1 Components of Member Equity

| Component | Who Earns It | Calculation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Capital Return** | Founders (Tier 3) with lump sums | `lump_sum × vesting_pct × min(1.0, pool / total_lumps)` — return of capital, capped at original contribution |
| **Common Equity** | All members | `(pool - total_lumps) × (weighted_member_months / total_weighted_months)` — time-weighted, not dollar-weighted |
| **Credit Limit** | All members | `min(total_contributions × 5, coverage × 0.75)` — 5× contributions, capped at 75% of death benefit |

### 4.2 Internal Capital Accounts
- Each member maintains an internal capital account
- Capital return tracked separately from common equity
- Monthly statements showing all components

### 4.3 Unequal Founder Contributions
- Different founder lump sum amounts permitted (no minimum — any lump sum welcome, gets higher credit limit)
- Capital return proportional to individual lump sum, not equal shares
- Example: Founder A ($200K) + Founder B ($100K) = Founder A gets 2× capital return
- All founders pay the same monthly contribution ($20/mo minimum)

### 4.4 Vesting Schedule
- Capital return: 20% per year, 5 years to full vest
- Common equity (from monthly contributions): always fully vested
- Vesting begins from date of first contribution
- Unvested equity remains in the cooperative pool on departure

### 4.5 Other Equity Provisions
- Limited equity cap on housing shares (prevents speculation)
- No guaranteed interest or return on lump sum contributions
- Founders earn through pool growth + time-weighted equity
- Annual equity statements provided to all members

### 4.6 Early Member Bonus
- Members joining in months 1-12 get 1.5× weight on member-months for common equity
- Rewards early participation and risk-taking during cooperative formation

### 4.7 Credit Limit Model
- Each $1 contributed unlocks $5 in credit, capped at 75% of death benefit
- Lump sums unlock credit immediately
- Monthly contributions accumulate credit over time
- Credit limit reviewed annually based on pool health

## Article V: Voting Rights

- One member, one vote (NOT dollar-weighted)
- Simple majority for operational decisions
- Supermajority (2/3) for:
  - Property acquisition
  - Amendments to operating agreement
  - Member admission/expulsion
  - Loans exceeding $100K
- Board of directors: 5-7 members, elected annually
- Tier 3 members eligible for guaranteed board seats (max 2)

## Article VI: Premium Allocation (Age-Based Fairness)

- Premiums calculated per individual member age bracket
- Group discount applied uniformly (30% off individual rates)
- Older members pay higher premiums (per actuarial tables)
- Premium allocation plan documented and approved by membership
- Annual review of premium costs and allocation
- Reserve for premium increases as members age

## Article VII: Reserve Requirements

- Minimum 15% of pool value kept liquid (high-yield savings)
- Operating reserve: 3 months of fixed expenses
- Property reserve: 6 months of mortgage payments
- Insurance premium reserve: 6 months of premiums prepaid
- Emergency fund: $10K minimum

## Article VIII: Subsidiary Entities

### Property Holding LLC
- Owns real estate
- Clean entity for lender underwriting
- Community Land Trust holds land (optional, for affordability)
- Ground lease connects CLT to cooperative

## Article IX: Distributions and Use of Funds

- Pool funds used for: down payments, operating expenses, insurance premiums
- Rental income from property returns to cooperative pool
- Net operating income reinvested or distributed per board decision
- No distributions that would impair reserve requirements
- Annual accounting and member statements

## Article X: Amendments

- Proposed in writing with 30-day notice
- Supermajority vote required
- Legal review for regulatory compliance changes
- Version tracking and member notification
