# Buy-Sell Agreement Outline

## Article I: Purpose and Scope

- Governs all member exit scenarios
- Ensures business continuity
- Protects remaining members and lender interests
- Prevents disputes over valuation and timing

## Article II: Triggering Events

### 2.1 Death
- Death benefit pays out to cooperative (as collateral assignee)
- Cooperative takes only outstanding loan share
- Remaining death benefit to member's family/beneficiaries
- Equity buyout funded from death benefit or cooperative reserves
- Timeline: 60 days from date of death

### 2.2 Disability
- Definition: unable to meet contribution obligations for 6+ months
- Disability insurance proceeds (if any) applied to contributions
- Cooperative may fund contributions during disability period (board discretion)
- If permanent: treated as voluntary departure after 12 months
- Equity protected during disability period

### 2.3 Retirement/Lifecycle Buyout (Tier 3)
- Capital return (vested portion of lump sum) + common equity
- No cash value component in term-only model
- Minimum 5-year membership before lifecycle buyout eligible
- 90-day notice required
- Capital return processed from cooperative pool

### 2.4 Voluntary Departure
- 60-day written notice required
- Vested equity returned (per vesting schedule: 20%/year over 5 years)
- Unvested portion remains in pool
- Collateral assignment released upon full exit
- No penalty beyond unvested equity
- Housing member must vacate unit within 90 days

### 2.5 Default/Forced Buyout
- Triggered after 90-day cure period for missed contributions
- Board vote required (supermajority)
- Vested equity returned minus amounts owed
- Collateral assignment released
- Housing member must vacate within 90 days
- Credit bureau notification of default

## Article III: Valuation Formula

### 3.1 Founder Exit (Tier 3)
- **Capital return**: `lump_sum × vesting_pct × min(1.0, pool / total_lumps)`
  - Only vested portion is paid out
  - Pool coverage ratio protects cooperative if pool has declined
  - Capped at original contribution (return of capital, not profit)
- **Common equity**: `(pool - total_lumps) × (weighted_member_months / total_weighted_months)`
  - Always fully vested (earned from monthly contributions, time-weighted)
- **Exit amount**: vested capital return + common equity − outstanding obligations

### 3.2 Regular Member Exit (Tier 1/2)
- **Common equity**: `(pool - total_lumps) × (weighted_member_months / total_weighted_months)`
  - Always fully vested, time-weighted by member-months of participation
- **Exit amount**: common equity − outstanding obligations

### 3.3 Unequal Founder Amounts
- Founders with different lump sums receive proportional capital return
- Example: Founder A ($200K lump) gets 2× the capital return of Founder B ($100K lump)
- No guaranteed interest on lump sums — returns come from pool growth
- Year 3 exit example (60% vested):
  - Founder A: ~$120K capital return + common equity − owed
  - Founder B: ~$60K capital return + common equity − owed

### 3.4 Large Exit Funding
- If capital return exceeds available pool: installment payments over 36 months
- Installment payments accrue no interest (founder already earned through pool)
- Board may negotiate shorter or longer terms based on pool health
- Life insurance proceeds (death) always paid in full within 60 days

### 3.5 Other Adjustments
- Plus: pro-rata appreciation on property (limited equity cap)
- Minus: outstanding obligations (loans), unvested portion
- Minus: pro-rata share of cooperative liabilities
- Independent valuation for disputes (cost split between parties)

## Article IV: Funding Mechanisms

### Primary: Life Insurance
- Death: death benefit covers buyout + loan share
- Living buyout: vested capital return from pool

### Secondary: Cooperative Reserves
- Reserve fund covers gaps between death benefit and obligations
- Installment payments allowed if reserves insufficient (max 36 months)

### Funding Gap Provisions (per lender analysis Table III)
- If death benefit < equity stake: gap funded from reserves
- If reserves insufficient: installment plan with interest
- If capital return > available pool (Tier 3): partial buyout + installment
- Annual review of funding adequacy
- Board authority to require additional insurance if gaps detected

## Article V: Process and Timeline

1. Triggering event occurs
2. Written notice to board (or automatic for death)
3. Valuation calculated per Article III
4. Board confirms valuation within 30 days
5. Payment within 60 days (or installment plan)
6. Collateral assignment released
7. Membership terminated
8. Slot opened for new member admission

## Article VI: Dispute Resolution

1. Good faith negotiation (30 days)
2. Mediation (60 days, cost split)
3. Binding arbitration (per AAA rules)
4. Litigation only if arbitration fails

## Article VII: Restrictions on Transfer

- Membership interests are non-transferable
- No assignment of membership to third parties
- Housing shares may only be sold back to cooperative (limited equity)
- No encumbrance of membership interests
